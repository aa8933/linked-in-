Competing Calls-to-Action and Cognitive Overload in New User Onboarding

Case Study:

Problem Statement: New LinkedIn users are bombarded with a barrage of action calls and profile tasks simultaneously, such as "Available for work," "Add a section," and "Improve profile." The lack of a visual hierarchy and the abundance of options lead to visual clutter and increased cognitive load. As a result, users may struggle to prioritize the most important next step, leading to distraction, slower profile completion, and potentially even abandonment.

Cause:

Too many action calls are displayed at once.

Lack of a clear visual hierarchy and prioritization.

The presence of competing elements vying for the user's attention simultaneously.

Impact:

Increased cognitive load.

Decision fatigue.

Reduced focus on essential setup tasks.

 Slower profile completion.

 Increased likelihood of abandoning the task. Violation of a User Experience Principle.

UX Principle Violated

Aesthetic and minimalist design (according to Nielsen guidelines).

Heck's Law: Too many choices prolong decision-making.

Visual hierarchy principles: Lack of clarity regarding the prioritization of important actions.
